Culture & festivals
🥁
Kajari Mahotsav
Folk music festival
The biggest festival in Mirzapur — women sing soulful Kajari songs in the monsoon, lamenting separation and celebrating nature.
🪅
Jhulan Utsav
Swing festival · Jul–Aug
A city-wide swing festival in which the entire community participates in colour, devotion, and celebration.
🪔
Navratri
Nine-day goddess festival
Millions descend on Vindhyavasini Temple. Special pujas, aartis, and cultural programmes transform Vindhyachal into a city of light.
🎵
Biraha folk music
Oral poetry tradition
Improvised poetry and song — singers recount mythological epics and tales of longing in an ancient form unique to this region.
🌊
Chhath Puja
Ganga ghat ritual
Predawn rituals and floating lamps at the ghats create an unforgettable scene of collective devotion at the holy river.
🧵
Carpet & brassware craft
Living artisan heritage
Hand-knotted carpets and intricate brassware are crafted by artisan families whose techniques span generations.

Litti Chokha₹50–80
Baked wheat balls with roasted gram, served with smoky mashed brinjal and potato chokha.
Baati Chokha₹60–100
Hard-baked dough balls dunked in ghee, served with chokha — a dhaba staple across Mirzapur.
Puri Sabzi₹40–70
Deep-fried puffy bread with spiced potato sabzi — the staple breakfast of Mirzapur's old city.
Kachori₹15–25
Crispy fried pastry filled with spiced lentils. Best eaten fresh off the tawa near the ghats.
Chaat & Samosa₹20–40
Tangy spiced street snacks piled with sev, tamarind chutney, and curd near the temple complex.
Jalebi₹20–40/100g
Spiral fried swirls soaked in saffron sugar syrup — the iconic Mirzapur sweet, best eaten warm.
Rabri & Malai₹30–60
Slow-cooked condensed milk with cream layers — a luxurious dessert at temple town sweet shops.
